1- Anthracnose Colletotrichum gloeosporiddes
  • Fungi infect young & mature fruits.
  • but in young fruits, symptoms appear when ripen.
  • Lesions can be on the fruit skin or invade & darken the flesh to store.
  • Salmon-Pink coloured spores on the lesions under high RH conditions.
  • Site selection.
  • Cultivar selection.
2- Stem-end rot
  • Lasiodiploidia theobromae
    (Synonym-Botryodiploidia theobromae)
  • Fungus grows from pedical into a circular black lesion arround the pedicel.
  • Later,fungus grons unevenly with advancing margin of the lesion.
  • Harvesting on a clear dry day.
  • Fruits should be covered and removed to the ripening houses immediately after havest.
